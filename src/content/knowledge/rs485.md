# RS485 Wiring for Modbus RTU Devices

RS485 is the physical communication layer used by many Modbus RTU devices. It is common in energy meters, VFDs, water instruments, solar inverters, and Remote IO modules.

For industrial IoT projects, RS485 details matter because wiring quality directly affects whether a gateway can read Modbus data reliably.

## Practical RS485 Checklist

Before connecting a gateway or RTU, confirm:

- All devices use the same baud rate, parity, stop bits, and Modbus address plan.
- The RS485 pair is wired consistently across the bus.
- The bus topology is kept clean, with unnecessary stubs avoided.
- Termination and biasing follow the final hardware and site requirements.
- Shielding and grounding are reviewed for the cabinet environment.

Do not use a generic RS485 article as a final wiring standard. The actual wiring method should follow the device datasheet, installation manual, and site electrical rules.

## RS485 and Industrial IoT Gateways

An industrial gateway can poll RS485 Modbus RTU devices, convert selected registers into normalized telemetry, and publish the data to MQTT or a dashboard.

The [IEG-100 Ethernet Industrial IoT Gateway](/products/ieg-100-ethernet-industrial-iot-gateway) is the IoTEdges public draft for Ethernet-based gateway deployments where RS485 Modbus data is collected from field devices.

## RS485 and Remote IO

Remote IO modules often use RS485 Modbus RTU so a gateway, RTU, or controller can read distributed DI/DO/AI/AO signals.

The [IEIO-100 Modbus Remote IO Module](/products/ieio-100-modbus-remote-io-module) is planned as a wired Modbus Remote IO family. Exact RS485 isolation, surge, ESD, register map, and electrical limits should remain unpublished until validation passes.

## RS485 and RTUs

RTUs can combine local IO and RS485 communication in the same cabinet. This makes them useful for pump stations, water systems, factory cabinets, and small remote monitoring panels.

The [IER-100 Ethernet Industrial RTU](/products/ier-100-ethernet-industrial-rtu) is the current IoTEdges public draft for local IO plus wired telemetry.

## Validation Notes

RS485 performance depends on wiring, device count, baud rate, noise environment, grounding, and firmware polling behavior. Keep exact point counts and distance claims validation-gated until prototype and field tests are complete.
